# Code of Conduct

## 🌍 Our Commitment

We are committed to fostering an open, inclusive, and respectful environment for everyone contributing to this project.

This project is intended to be a safe space for collaboration, learning, and professional growth — regardless of experience level, background, or identity.

---

## 🤝 Expected Behavior

All participants are expected to:

- Be respectful and professional in all interactions
- Provide constructive and helpful feedback
- Be open to different viewpoints and experiences
- Support and guide new contributors
- Focus on what is best for the project and community

---

## 🚫 Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not tolerated:

- Harassment, discrimination, or hate speech of any kind
- Personal attacks, insults, or aggressive communication
- Trolling or deliberately disruptive behavior
- Sharing private or sensitive information without consent
- Spam, self-promotion, or irrelevant content

---

## 🛡️ Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for:

- Monitoring community behavior
- Clarifying standards when needed
- Taking appropriate and fair corrective actions

---

## ⚖️ Enforcement Actions

If a contributor violates this Code of Conduct, maintainers may take actions such as:

- Warning the contributor
- Removing or editing content (comments, commits, issues)
- Temporarily restricting participation
- Permanent ban from the project

Severity and consequences will be determined based on the situation.

---

## 📍 Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project-related spaces, including:

- GitHub repositories
- Issues and Pull Requests
- Code reviews
- Discussions and community channels
